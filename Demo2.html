Vigan City is a charming and historical city located in the province of Ilocos Sur, Philippines. Known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, it offers a glimpse into the country's rich past. One of its main attractions is Calle Crisologo, a narrow cobblestone street lined with beautiful ancestral houses that evoke a sense of nostalgia. The city is also home to Plaza Salcedo, a peaceful public square surrounded by significant landmarks, such as St. Paul's Metropolitan Cathedral and the Provincial Capitol. Visitors can explore museums like the Syquia Mansion Museum, which showcases historical artifacts, or enjoy the interactive zoo and wildlife sanctuary at Baluarte. Additionally, the Bantay Church and Bell Tower provide a picturesque setting and excellent views of the surrounding countryside. In Vigan City, visitors can immerse themselves in the city's heritage, wander through its streets, indulge in local cuisine, and truly experience the unique charm and beauty of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Vigan City is a charming and historic city located in Ilocos Sur, Philippines. It is renowned for its well-preserved Spanish colonial past, earning it recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city's cobblestone streets, ancestral houses, and horse-drawn carriages create a picturesque atmosphere that transports visitors back in time.

Exploring Vigan City is like taking a journey through history. The iconic Calle Crisologo is lined with beautifully preserved Spanish-era houses, some of which have been converted into museums, souvenir shops, and restaurants. Visitors can also visit the famous St. Paul's Metropolitan Cathedral and the nearby Plaza Salcedo, named after Juan de Salcedo, the Spanish conquistador who founded the city.

PLACES TO VISIT IN VIGAN CITY
Calle Crisologo: This iconic street is lined with cobblestone paths and beautifully preserved ancestral houses, giving visitors a glimpse into the city's colonial past. Take a leisurely walk along Calle Crisologo, admire the Spanish-style architecture, and explore the various shops selling local handicrafts and souvenirs.
Plaza Salcedo: Situated in the heart of the city, Plaza Salcedo is a public square named after the Spanish conquistador Juan de Salcedo. It features a statue of Salcedo at its center and is surrounded by notable landmarks such as the St. Paul's Metropolitan Cathedral and the Provincial Capitol.
St. Paul's Metropolitan Cathedral: This impressive church is the focal point of religious activities in Vigan City. The cathedral's imposing facade and intricate interior design showcase a blend of European and Filipino architectural styles. Attend a mass or simply enjoy the serene ambiance while appreciating the religious art and sculptures inside.
BALUARTE: Owned by former Ilocos Sur Governor Chavit Singson, Baluarte is a mini zoo that offers visitors the opportunity to interact with various animals. From tigers and crocodiles to exotic birds and domesticated farm animals, Baluarte provides an enjoyable experience for nature lovers and animal enthusiasts.
Bantay Church and Bell Tower: Located just outside the city proper, Bantay Church is a picturesque church complex that dates back to the 16th century. Its bell tower, known as the Bantay Bell Tower, stands separately from the church and offers a stunning panoramic view of Vigan City and its surroundings.
Vigan Heritage Village: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Vigan Heritage Village showcases Filipino-Spanish architecture at its finest. Take a stroll through its narrow streets, admire the well-preserved ancestral houses, and indulge in local delicacies such as empanadas and Vigan longganisa.
